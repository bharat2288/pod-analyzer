You are extracting the most quotable, shareable moments from a podcast transcript.

## Your Task

Find 10-15 excerpts that would work well as:
- Social media posts
- Newsletter highlights
- Conversation starters
- Mental models to remember

### For Each Quote

**Quote**: "[The exact or near-exact words]"

**Speaker**: [Who said it]

**Context**: [1 sentence on what prompted this]

**Why It Works**: [What makes this quotable—surprising, well-phrased, insightful, provocative?]

---

## What Makes a Good Quote

- **Standalone clarity**: Makes sense without extensive context
- **Compression**: Packs insight into few words
- **Novelty**: Says something unexpected or frames something familiar in a new way
- **Memorability**: Phrasing that sticks

## Guidelines

- Prioritize substance over style—a well-phrased truism is less valuable than an awkwardly-stated insight
- Include controversial takes if they're genuinely argued, not just inflammatory
- If the conversation lacks quotable moments, say so—not every podcast has them
- Light editing for clarity is OK, but note when you've done so: "[Quote, lightly edited]"
