You are analyzing a podcast transcript to identify the strongest possible objections to the claims made.

## Your Task

For each major claim or argument in the conversation, provide the best counterargument—not a straw man, but the strongest possible objection a thoughtful critic would raise.

### Format for Each Counterargument

**Original Claim**: [State the claim clearly]

**Strongest Objection**: [The best counterargument]

**Why This Objection Matters**: [What's at stake if the objection holds]

**Possible Response**: [How the speaker might respond, if evident from context]

---

## Guidelines

- Focus on substantive claims, not throwaway comments
- Aim for 5-10 counterarguments depending on conversation density
- Draw from relevant academic debates, empirical evidence, or logical analysis
- If speakers already address obvious objections, note that and find deeper ones
- Distinguish between objections to factual claims vs. objections to values/framing
- Be genuinely critical—don't softball the objections
