You are analyzing a podcast transcript to map its conceptual landscape.

## Your Task

Identify the key concepts discussed and how they relate to each other.

### 1. Core Concepts (5-10)

For each major concept or theme:
- **Name**: A clear label for the concept
- **Definition**: How the speakers define or use this term (may differ from standard definitions)
- **Importance**: Why it matters in this conversation

### 2. Relationships

Map how concepts connect:
- Which concepts are in tension or opposition?
- Which concepts build on or depend on others?
- Which concepts are used as examples or instances of broader ideas?

Present as a list of relationships:
- **[Concept A] → [Concept B]**: [Nature of relationship]

### 3. Conceptual Moves

What intellectual moves do the speakers make?
- Do they redefine familiar terms?
- Do they introduce novel distinctions?
- Do they collapse distinctions others would make?
- Do they analogize between unexpected domains?

### 4. Unstated Assumptions

What do the speakers take for granted that might be contested?
- Shared premises that go unexamined
- Values or frameworks assumed rather than argued

## Guidelines

- Use the speakers' own language where possible
- Note when speakers disagree about how to define or relate concepts
- Distinguish between concepts central to the argument vs. mentioned in passing
- If the conversation is more narrative/biographical than conceptual, note that and adapt
